Combine in spray bottle. Apply after DC rinse — work into scalp and through lengths. Leave 2–3 min. Rinse thoroughly with cool water. Do not leave on — 1:8 ratio is safe, undiluted is not.
Why ACV works: Healthy hair pH is 4.5–5.5. Most shampoos and treatments push hair to pH 7–9 (alkaline), which raises the cuticle and causes frizz, tangles, and color fade. ACV (pH 3) restores the acid mantle and physically closes the cuticle.

Green tea: Catechins in green tea inhibit DHT production at the follicle level. Combined with the rinse, it's a functional scalp treatment alongside the pH balance.

Nightly Protection

Every single night without exception

Satin Bonnet (required)JBCO (edges)Leven Rose Jojoba (scalp)
Pineapple: Gather all hair loosely at crown with a soft scrunchie — never elastics. This preserves curl pattern overnight without compression.
Cotton vs. Satin: Cotton pillowcases have a friction coefficient of 3.7 vs. satin's 0.9. That's 4× more friction on your hair shaft every time you move overnight. Over weeks: significant cuticle damage, breakage, and moisture loss. Non-negotiable.
Days 3–4: If your wash & go starts looking tired, co-wash (conditioner only, no shampoo) to refresh without stripping. Camille Rose Rosemary Water can extend this by a day as a midday refresher.

Weekly Scalp Cleanse

Even with style in — don't skip

Max duration: 6–8 weeks. After that, new growth matting at the roots will begin to loc. Set a takedown reminder at week 7.
Dilute shampoo 1:3 with water in applicator bottle. Apply directly to scalp, not the style itself. Massage scalp, then rinse letting water run through the style. Follow with diluted ACV rinse. Air dry completely before re-oiling.
Why scalp cleanse still matters: Sebum buildup + product buildup creates a biofilm over follicle openings within 2–3 weeks. This physically blocks new growth. Scalp odor in protective styles is a sign this is happening.

Boil water, add flaxseeds. Stir constantly. Watch for foam ring to form and gel to coat the back of a spoon — 7–10 min. Do not over-cook. Strain immediately through cheesecloth, squeeze hard to extract all gel. Seeds discarded. While warm: stir in glycerin, PQ-7, EOs. Cool, pour into squeeze bottle. Refrigerate — 1–2 weeks. Polyquaternium-7 (PQ-7): Quaternary ammonium polymer — the active ingredient in most commercial curl-defining gels. You own the raw material. Adding it to flaxseed gel gives you professional-grade frizz control at a fraction of the cost.

Chia seed variant: ¼ cup chia seeds in 2 cups water, soak overnight — no boiling needed. Even thicker gel. Can be combined 50/50 with flaxseed gel for intermediate hold.
04

Drying

Air dry or diffuse — never scrunch while wet

Oribe Impermeable Anti-Humidity Spray (when dry)AllPlex No.7 Bonding Oil (finishing)Amika Glass Action Elixir (finish)
Diffuse technique: Low heat, low speed. Cup curls in diffuser, hover — do not scrunch. Diffuse to 80% dry then air dry the rest. Scrunching while wet pulls curl sections apart, breaking clumps and creating frizz. Wait until you can scrunch and feel no water.
01

Start on Damp Hair

60–70% dry is ideal

ORS Super Silken Leave-InCamille Rose Almond Jai ButterCurls Blueberry Bliss JellyPhyto Joba Gel
Moisture + cream ratio: Apply leave-in first to re-wet the section slightly. Then a thin layer of twisting butter. The cream gives grip for the twist pattern to hold definition. Too much cream = undefined, "stringy" unravel. Too little = frizzy, undefined.
02

Twist Tightly

Tight twist = more defined unravel

Two-strand twist each section. For most definition: smooth hair flat with comb first before twisting. Seal ends by finger-coiling or placing on a perm rod. The smaller the twist, the tighter the unravel pattern. Twist in the same direction each time for consistency.
03

Dry Completely Before Unraveling

This is the most important rule

Never unravel while damp. Twist-outs set while drying — the pattern is literally being formed by the hydrogen bonds realigning as water evaporates. Unravel while wet and the hydrogen bonds haven't set, creating frizz.
30–45 min under hooded dryer OR overnight air dry. Test: press one twist between your fingers — if it's still cool to the touch, it's still damp inside. Cool = moisture. Warm or room temp = dry and ready.
04

Unravel with Oiled Fingers

From ends up — never rush

Leven Rose Jojoba (on fingers)Bio Silk Silk Therapy (anti-frizz)Amika Glass Action Elixir
Coat fingertips with a small amount of jojoba. Separate the twist from the ends upward, gently. Never pull from the root — this creates immediate frizz at the root section. Work slowly. The patience here directly determines how long the style lasts.

Pre-Install Prep

Always wash & DC before installing protective style

Full wash day protocolProtein DC (if in rotation)Full LOC methodBy Taraji Hot Commodity Heat Protectant (if blowout)Oribe Balm d'Or (heat shield)
Why full wash before install: Once in a protective style, your hair is trapped for 6–8 weeks. Any dryness, damage, or protein imbalance is locked in and compounds over that time. Installed clean and well-moisturized, the style will protect; installed dry or damaged, it will break.
!

Tension Rules — Non-Negotiable

Traction alopecia prevention

If it hurts when installed — it's too tight. Tell your stylist immediately. Speak up — this is your hair line at stake. Traction alopecia from repeated tight installs is one of the most common causes of permanent edge loss and is not reversible once scarring begins.
The frontal hairline follicles are more susceptible to mechanical stress than other areas. The inflammation from tight braiding accumulates over months — the edge recession you see is months of subclinical follicle stress made visible. One tight install won't do it; repeated installs will.
